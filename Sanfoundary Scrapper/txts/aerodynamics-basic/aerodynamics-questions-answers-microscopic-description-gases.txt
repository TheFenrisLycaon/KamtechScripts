1. At higher temperatures, the assumption of a calorically perfect gas is valid.
a) True
b) False
b
2. What is specific heat a function of at high temperatures?
a) Time
b) Temperature
c) Entropy
d) Density
b
3. At high temperatures, what does dissociation of molecule mean?
a) Atoms break away from molecular structure
b) Electrons break away from the atom
c) Protons and neutrons break away
d) Electrons jump to the higher energy state
a
4. At 4000 K what happens to the gas?
a) Oxygen is dissociated completely
b) Nitrogen is dissociated completely
c) Oxygen is dissociated completely, nitrogen begins to dissociate
d) Ionization of both oxygen and nitrogen
c

5. How many thermal degrees of freedom is there for a diatomic molecule having translation energy?
a) 2
b) 3
c) 4
d) 6
b
6. How many degrees of freedom is there for a CO2 molecule with rotational kinetic energy?
a) 1
b) 2
c) 3
d) 6
b
7. The source of electronic energy is kinetic energy due to translation motion.
a) True
b) False
a
8. For a single atom what is the total energy a sum of?
a) Translation and rotational energy
b) Electrical and vibrational energy
c) Translation and electronic energy
d) Rotational, electronic and translation energy
c
9. What is a ground state?
a) Energy when gas is at absolute zero
b) Energy when gas is at 273.15 K
c) State where energies are non – existent
d) State where only translation energy exists
a
10. In a boson particle, how many elementary particles are present inside a molecule?
a) Odd number
b) Even number
c) Zero
d) Imaginary number
b
11. What are the particles which obey Fermi – Dirac statistics called?
a) Bosons
b) Fermions
c) Leptons
d) Quarks
