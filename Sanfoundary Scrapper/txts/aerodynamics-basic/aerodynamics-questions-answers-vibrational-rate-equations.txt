1. What is the value of transition probability?
a) 0
b) More than 1
c) Less than 1
d) 1
c
2. What does the product of collision frequency and transition probability yield?
a) Number of transitions per particle per second
b) Number of collisions per second
c) Number of collisions per second per particle
d) Transitions per collision
a
3. What is the formula to compute net rate change of the population of the ith level?
a) \(\frac {dN_i}{dt}\) = Pi + 1, i ZNi + 1 + Pi – 1, i ZNi – 1 – Pi, i + 1 ZNi – Pi, i – 1 ZNi
b) \(\frac {dN_i}{dt}\) = – Pi + 1, i ZNi + 1 + Pi – 1, i ZNi – 1 + Pi, i + 1 ZNi + Pi, i – 1 ZNi
c) \(\frac {dN_i}{dt}\) = Pi + 1, i ZNi + 1 – Pi, i + 1 ZNi
d) \(\frac {dN_i}{dt}\) = + Pi – 1, i ZNi – 1 – Pi, i – 1 ZNi
a
\(\frac {dN_i}{dt}\) = Pi + 1, i ZNi + 1 + Pi – 1, i ZNi – 1 – Pi, i + 1 ZNi – Pi, i – 1 ZNi
Where, Pi + 1, i ZNi + 1 + Pi – 1, i ZNi – 1 is the rate of increase of population in ith level due to the molecules jumping up from i – 1 and down from i + 1 levels.
Pi, i + 1 ZNi – Pi, i – 1 ZNi is the rate of decrease of population in the ith level due to the molecules jumping from ith level to i + 1 and i – 1 levels.

b) \(\frac {dN_i}{dt}\) = ki + 1, i Ni + 1 + ki – 1, i Ni – 1 – ki, i + 1Ni – ki, i – 1 Ni

d) \(\frac {dN_i}{dt}\) = Pi + 1, i ZNi + 1 + Pi – 1, i ZNi – 1
b
\(\frac {dN_i}{dt}\) = ki + 1, i Ni + 1 + ki – 1, i Ni – 1 – ki, i + 1Ni – ki, i – 1 Ni
