1. The amount of nickel present in Inconel is __________
a) 0.2%
b) 0.25%
c) 46%
d) 79.5%
d
2. At normal temperatures, the material K Monel is __________
a) magnetic
b) active
c) non-magnetic
d) corrosion prone
c
3. The melting point of Monel is around __________
a) 1220°F
b) 2355-2566°C
c) 2370-2460°F
d) 4500°F
c
4. Monel resembles mild steel in a few working properties.
a) True
b) False
a
5. The melting point of Inconel is ___________
a) 1090°F
b) 2540°F
c) 2900°F
d) 2540°C
b
6. The approximate amount of nickel present in Monel is __________
a) 1%
b) 0.15%
c) 67%
d) 33%
c
7. It is possible to harden Inconel by heat treatment.
a) True
b) False
b
8. The approximate amount of copper present in K Monel is __________
a) 0.85%
b) 0.9%
c) 46%
d) 29%
d
9. Inconel ____________ high strength and high temperatures.
a) generates
b) radiates
c) preserves
d) hooks
c
10. The density of K Monel is __________
a) 90.34 grams per cubic centimetre
b) 44 grams per cubic centimetre
c) 18.2 grams per cubic centimetre
d) 8.47 grams per cubic centimetre
d
11. Monel can be welded ___________
a) rarely
b) easily
c) with difficulty
d) timelessly
b
12. ___________ alleviates internal stress.
a) Annealing
b) Conducting
c) Bombarding
d) Inundating
a
13. ___________ is not a method of welding of Monel.
a) Oxyacetylene
b) Cold-stiffening
c) Carbon-arc welding
d) Metallic-arc welding
b
14. Heating of alloys that have high nickel content should be done in __________ atmospheres.
a) sulphur free
b) low temperatures
c) sulphur rich
d) sulphur infused
a
15. Inconel is softened by heating up to ___________
a) 1800°C
b) 1800°F
c) 180°C
d) 180°F
b
