1. What is the full form of VOR?
a) Virtual Omni-Directional Range
b) VHF Omni-Directional Range
c) VHF Omni-Directing Range
d) VHF Omni-Directional Radar
b
2. VOR is a _________ range radio navigation method.
a) Short
b) Ultra long
c) Long
d) Medium
a
3. The line of position is called the ________ from the VOR.
a) Diagonal
b) Axial
c) Radial
d) Lateral
c
4. The VOR uses frequencies in which of the following band?
a) LF
b) MF
c) VHF
d) UHF
c
5. What is the full form of RDF?
a) Radial direction finding
b) Radio direction finding
c) Rate direction finding
d) Ratio direction finding
b
6. Where was the VOR developed?
a) Australia
b) Germany
c) India
d) The United States
d
7. The extremely directional second signal sent by a VOR ground station generally rotates in a clockwise direction in space for about ________ times a second.
a) 30
b) 3
c) 50
d) 5
a
8. The signals transmitted from VOR stations are competent up to __________
a) 4,000 miles
b) 2,000 miles
c) 200 miles
d) 10,000 miles
c
9. VOR and NDB approaches are being replaced by _________
a) RADAR
b) RNAV
c) TACAN
d) CAT
b
10. VOR signals provide much greater accuracy as compared to NDB systems.
a) False
b) True
b
11. How much is the predictable accuracy of VOR signals?
a) 900 km
b) 90 km
c) 90 m
d) 900 m
c
12. VOR stations depend on __________
a) Line of approach
b) Line of sight
c) Line of descent
d) Line of attack
b
13. VHF radio is more vulnerable to diffraction.
a) False
b) True
a
