1. The phenomenon of twisting around itself by a molecule to relieve helical stress is ____________
a) Supercoiling
b) Coiling
c) Elongation
d) Compression
a
2. When there is no net bending of the DNA axis upon itself, the DNA is said to be in a ____________
a) Elongated state
b) Compressed state
c) Relaxed state
d) Denatured state
c
3. Over-twisting of a molecule results in ____________
a) Negative supercoiling
b) Positive supercoiling
c) Elongation
d) Compression
b
4. Changing twist from the relaxed state requires adding energy and increases the ____________
a) Forces in molecule
b) Stress along molecule
c) Strain over molecule
d) None of the above
b
5. The enzyme responsible for the removal of supercoiling in replicating DNA ahead of the replication fork is ____________
a) Topoisomerase
b) Primase
c) DNA polymerase
d) Helicase
a
6. The type of topoisomerases which generally relaxes DNA by removing negative supercoiling?
a) Type I
b) Type II
c) Type III
d) Type IV
a
7. The type of topoisomerases that can introduce negative supercoils is ____________
a) Type I
b) Type II
c) Type III
d) Type IV
b
8. The type I enzyme(s) is/are ____________
a) Topoisomerases I and III
b) Topoisomerases II
c) Topoisomerase IV
d) Topoisomerases I and IV
a
9. Under-winding is measured in terms of ____________
a) Supercoiling
b) Linking difference
c) Positive supercoiling
d) Negative supercoiling
b
10. Which type of supercoiling takes the form of extended right-handed coils?
a) Plectonemic supercoiling
b) Solenoidal supercoiling
c) Negative supercoiling
d) Positive supercoiling
a
