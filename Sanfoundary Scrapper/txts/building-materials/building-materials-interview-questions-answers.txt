1. Construction of cavity walls as external walls to protect the building from the outside heat or cold as the hollow space between two walls act as an ________
a) Burning material
b) Insulating material
c) Destructive material
d) Building material
b
2. __________ in a building means the free passage of clean air in a building.
a) Habitation
b) Protection
c) Sanitation
d) Ventilation
d
3. Which gas leads to suffocation and dizziness to the occupants when an increase in its quantity in the air.
a) Carbon monoxide
b) Hydrogen
c) Nitrogen
d) Carbon dioxide
d
4. The termite, which are responsible for the destruction of wooden materials, are popularly known as _______
a) Red ants
b) White ants
c) Black ants
d) Big ants
b
5. The science which deals with the sound insulation in a building is known as ___________
a) Reverberation
b) Transmission
c) Acoustic
d) Air borne
c
6. Fire extinguisher extinguishes the small fires in the house bye using ___________
a) Carbon dioxide
b) Nitrogen gas
c) Water
d) Foam
a
7. The process in which the difference of temperature between the outside air and the inside air tends to the deposition of moisture on the room surfaces is known as ____________
a) Saturation
b) Accumulation
c) Precipitation
d) Condensation
d
8. The treatment given to the roof of a building to prevent the roof from the leakage of water is known as _________
a) Fire proofing
b) Damp proofing
c) Termite proofing
d) Sound proofing
b
9. _________ is a flexible material which is commonly used for the damp proofing.
a) Hot bitumen
b) Bituminous felts
c) Mastic asphalt
d) Metal sheets
a
10. Which one of the given below is a chemical insecticide for termite proofing?
a) Dieldrin
b) Sulphuric acid
c) Rodenticide
d) Pyrethrins
a
