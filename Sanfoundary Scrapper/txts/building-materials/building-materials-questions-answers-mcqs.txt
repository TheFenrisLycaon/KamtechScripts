1. _____________ used to mean the regulation of admitting more or less sunshine in the room.
a) Elegance
b) Roominess
c) Circulation
d) Aspect
d
2. The term ____________ is used to mean the link or access or movement between the various rooms and floors of building.
a) Flexibility
b) Prospect
c) Circulation
d) Elegance
c
3. _____________ is used to indicate the architectural effect produced by elevation in relation to width, height position of doors and windows, materials employed in the construction of external walls, etc.
a) Furniture requirements
b) Elegance
c) Roominess
d) Grouping
b
4. The placing of various rooms or units of a structure in a proper correlation of their functions and in due proximity with each other is known as ____________
a) Sanitation
b) Prospect
c) Economy
d) Grouping
d
5. One of the important requirements of good planning is ____________
a) Privacy
b) Furniture requirements
c) Prospect
d) Roominess
a
6. The term _____________ is used to refer the effect derived from space of a room that is its length, width and height.
a) Prospect
b) Sanitation
c) Privacy
d) Roominess
d
7. As a principle of planning, the term _______ is used to mean architectural hygiene.
a) Sanitation
b) Drainage
c) Water supply
d) Waste water
a
8. Due to improper ventilation, which gas gets stuck into house and develops dizziness to the occupants.
a) Oxygen
b) Nitrogen
c) Carbon dioxide
d) Hydrogen
c
9. The proper and sufficient ventilation results in absence of _________
a) Condensation
b) Deposition
c) Precipitation
d) Circulation
a
10. The ________ is required to control dust and other impurities in the air.
a) Circulation
b) Sanitation
c) Ventilation
d) Purification
c
