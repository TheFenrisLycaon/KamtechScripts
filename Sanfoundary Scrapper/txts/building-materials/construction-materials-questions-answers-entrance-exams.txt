1. The concrete is contained in a timber or steel casing for a certain period after its placing, this casing is known as the ____________
a) Tremie
b) Hopper
c) Shuttering
d) Grouting
c
2. A ___________ consists of a watertight pipe of diameter about 250 mm to 300 mm.
a) Hopper
b) Grout
c) Bucket
d) Tremie
d
3. A _____________ is provided at the top of the Tremie to receive the concrete.
a) Bucket
b) Hopper
c) Tremie
d) Pipe
b
4. __________ method is not in common use in case of placing concrete underwater.
a) Tremie
b) Grouting
c) Guniting
d) Spreading
a
5. The ____________ is the most effective process of repairing concrete work which has been damaged due to enquiry work or other reasons.
a) Grouting
b) Guniting
c) Vibrating
d) Pouring
b

6. The _________ is a mixture of cement and sand, the usual proposition being 1:3.
a) Grout
b) Mortar
c) Slurry
d) Gunite
d
7. It is observed that in cold weather, the curing is _________ in the initial stages of the setting of concrete.
a) Moderate
b) Slow
c) High
d) Fast
b
8. While placing concrete in hot weather, the aggregate should be _____________ to bring down the temperature of concrete.
a) Burnt
b) Pre cooled
c) Heated
d) Washed
b
9. In cold weather conditions, the temperature of concrete should not be allowed to fall below ___________ for at least 3 days after its placing.
a) 5° C
b) 25°C
c) 15°C
d) 50°C
c
10. The ___________ are open at top and their bottoms are provided with openable doors.
a) Tremie
b) Hopper
c) Buckets
d) Bevel
c
