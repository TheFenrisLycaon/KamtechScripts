1. For an open unsteady state system, 5 J of heat was supplied and 10 J of work was done on the system, what is the value of a change in enthalpy?
a)	5 J
b)	10 J
c)	15 J
d)	20 J
View answerAnswer: c
2. For an open unsteady state system, 10 J of heat was supplied and 10 J of work was done by the system, what is the value of a change in enthalpy?
a)	0
b)	10 J
c)	20 J
d)	– 20 J
View answerAnswer: a
3. For an open unsteady state system, 5 J of heat was supplied and 10 J of work was done by the system, what is the value of a change in enthalpy?
a)	– 5 J
b)	5 J
c)	10 J
d)	15 J
View answerAnswer: a
4. For an open unsteady state system, 15 J of heat was taken off and 10 J of work was done by the system, what is the value of a change in enthalpy?
a)	– 5 J
b)	5 J
c)	25 J
d)	– 25 J
View answerAnswer: a
5. For an open unsteady state system, 15 J of heat was supplied and -5 J of work was done on the system, what is the value of a change in enthalpy?
a)	10 J
b)	– 15 J
c)	20 J
d)	– 25 J
View answerAnswer: a
6. Which of the following represents vaporization?
a)	Change of vapor to liquid
b)	Change of vapor to solid
c)	Change of solid to vapor
d)	Change of liquid to vapor
View answerAnswer: d
7. Which of the following can definitely cause vaporization?
a)	Heating a liquid
b)	Heating a liquid at constant pressure
c)	Cooling a liquid
d)	Cooling a liquid at constant volume
View answerAnswer: b
8. Vaporization starts at which point?
a)	Dew point
b)	Bubble point
c)	Triple point
d)	None of the mentioned
View answerAnswer: b
9. What is the vaporization temperature of water at standard conditions?
a)	100oF
b)	212oF
c)	345oF
