1. What is curing?
a) Dehydration
b) Hydration
c) Drying
d) Dipping
b
2. In a dry environment, concrete strength will be loosed as much as _________ % in the moist environment.
a) 30
b) 40
c) 50
d) 60
c
3. Concrete placed in cold weather will take __________ time to gain strength.
a) No
b) Less
c) More
d) Equal to hot weather
c
4. After finishing concrete surface must be kept _________
a) Dry
b) First dry it and then wet it
c) First wet it and then dry it
d) Wet
d
5. Dry intervals in surface wetting leads to __________
a) Cracking
b) Fogging
c) High strength
d) Good workability
a
6. Contractors place blankets over the concrete to ____________
a) Increase the rate of evaporation
b) Slow the rate of evaporation
c) To increase the strength
d) Ease to do work
b
7. Well cured concrete _________ thermal, plastic & drying shrinkage cracks.
a) Doesn’t affect
b) Maximize
c) Minimize
d) Create
c
8. Which method is the most common and cheaper for water curing?
a) Ponding
b) Sprinkling
c) Mist curing
d) Wet covering
a
9. The hydration that provides the initial mix and chemical bond of the concrete’s ingredients is the _________
a) First step
b) Second step
c) Third step
d) Fourth step
a
10. What is the full form of rH?
a) Rhesus factor
b) Relative humidity
c) Rush hour
d) Radio head
b
