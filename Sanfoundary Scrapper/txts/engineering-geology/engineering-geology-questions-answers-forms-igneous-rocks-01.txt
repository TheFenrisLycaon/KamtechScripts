1. Form of igneous rock where magma is injected and cooled along planes of host rocks is ______________
a) Discordant bodies
b) Concordant bodies
c) Parallel bodies
d) Synchronous bodies
b
2. Pick the type of concordant body among the following.
a) Batholith
b) Extrusion
c) Dykes
d) Phacolith
d
3. The type of concordant body whose thickness is much smaller than its length and width is ______________
a) Sill
b) Phacolith
c) Dyke
d) Laccolith
a
4. The upper and lower margins of sills are relatively ______________
a) Coarser grain size
b) Medium grain size
c) Finer grain size
d) Can be of any size
c
5. Sills in length are restricted to hundreds of metres.
a) True
b) False
b
6. Most common rocks composing the sills are ______________
a) Marbles
b) Shales
c) Gabbros
d) Granites
c
7. The small sized intrusives that occupy bends in the folds are called ______________
a) Sills
b) Lopolith
c) Laccolith
d) Phacolith
d
8. The concordant bodies associated with structural basins are ______________
a) Sills
b) Lopolith
c) Laccolith
d) Phacolith
b
9. The shape arch or dome is shown by which type of concordant body?
a) Sills
b) Lopolith
c) Laccolith
d) Phacolith
c
10. What is the type of lava which leads to the formation of laccolith?
a) Viscous
b) Non-viscous
c) Partially crystallized
d) Gaseous lava
a
11. What is the type of laccolith in which fracture is formed?
a) Dyke
b) Batholith
c) Bysmalith
d) Volcanic neck
c
12. A sill is not considered to be a sill when two or more injections of different types of magma are involved.
a) True
b) False
b
