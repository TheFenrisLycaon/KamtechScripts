1. The fault not belonging to the classification of fault with the attitude of fault as basis ___________
a) Heave fault
b) Strike fault
c) Dip fault
d) Oblique fault
a
2. Faults that are developed along bedding planes are ___________
a) Strike faults
b) Dip faults
c) Bedding faults
d) Hade faults
c
3. The fault where fault strike is parallel to the dip of the layers broken and disrupted by the fault?
a) Dip faults
b) Strike faults
c) Oblique faults
d) Hade faults
a
4. The fault which is also called a diagonal fault is ___________
a) Wrench fault
b) Transform fault
c) Oblique fault
d) Dip fault
c
5. The type of fault which is observed in both continental and oceanic environment is ___________
a) Strike-slip fault
b) Dip fault
c) Oblique fault
d) Wrench fault
a
6. Which is the type of strike-slip fault in which the fault plane has developed transverse to the regional structure?
a) Transform fault
b) Wrench fault
c) Translational fault
d) Tear fault
b
7. Transverse fault is the other name for __________ fault.
a) Transform fault
b) Tear fault
c) Wrench fault
d) Normal fault
c
8. Where do the transform faults occur extensively?
a) Continental blocks
b) Oceanic ridges
c) Island blocks
d) Volcanic ridges
b
9. Identify the group of small sized faults from the following.
a) Parallel faults
b) Enechelon faults
c) Peripheral faults
d) Radial faults
b
10. Group of faults which appear emerging outward from a common central region are called ___________
a) Enechelon faults
b) Parallel faults
c) Peripheral faults
d) Radial faults
d
11. Parallel can sometimes lead to step faults.
a) True
b) False
a
12. The type of fault not belonging to the classification based on the mode of occurrence is ___________
a) Parallel fault
b) Peripheral fault
c) Enechelon fault
d) Wrench fault
d
