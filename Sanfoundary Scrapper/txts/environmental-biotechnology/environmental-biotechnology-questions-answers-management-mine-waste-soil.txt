1. Which of the following methods does not lead to the generation of mine wastes?
a) Extraction of minerals
b) Beneficiation of minerals
c) Processing of minerals
d) Remediation of mine waste
d
2. The first phase that leads to removal of ore is called as _______
a) Extraction
b) Beneficiation
c) Processing
d) Remediation
a
3. The materials that are left after the successful separation of the valuable fraction from the ore is called as Tailings.
a) True
b) False
a
4. Tailings suspended in the water can damage the environment by releasing toxic metals.
a) True
b) False
a
5. Which of the following term is not used in place of the term “Tailings”?
a) Mine dumps
b) Culm dumps
c) Terra-coned
d) Mine drainage
d
6. Which of the following is not an after effects of the toxic metals released during tailings?
a) Erosion
b) Sinkholes
c) Contamination
d) Nutrient absorption
d
7. Which of the following compound is used in the extraction of gold mining?
a) Liquid Nitrogen
b) Cyanide
c) Oxygen
d) Ammonia
b
8. Tailings is an output of which phase of mining?
a) Extraction
b) Beneficiation
c) Processing
d) Remediation
b
9. Which of the following condition is caused due to tainings?
a) Acid mine drainage
b) Nutrient absorption
c) Air contamination
d) Afforestation
a
10. Mill tailings is radioactive.
a) True
b) False
a
11. Which of the following methods is not included for disposal of tailings methods?
a) Submarine Tailings Disposal
b) Dry sacking
c) Phytostabilisation
d) Afforestation
d
12. When the open pit mines are refilled with the tailings is called as ________
a) Pond storage
b) Extraction
c) Beneficiation
d) Processing
a
13. Mining industry can try to stabilize the environmental pollution by plantation.
a) True
b) False
a
14. Acaccia mangium is considered an ideal plant for bioreclamation of overburden dumps.
a) True
b) False
a
15. Phyto stabilization is a technique of remediation where plants are used for removal of tailings from the environment by sequestering pollutants in areas near the roots.
a) True
b) False
a
