1. Why is S. cerevisiae is used to derive butanol?
a) It has high growth rate
b) It can overcome toxicity
c) Changes its own cell wall composition in response to toxicity
d) High tolerance to butanol
d
2. Which fatty acids can’t be used as abiofuel directly?
a) Ionic nature of carbonyl groups
b) Hydrophobic nature
c) If degree of saturation is high
d) Variation of length
a
3. Which of these is not one of the main biomass feedstocks?
a) Sugar
b) Starch
c) Lignocellulose
d) Hemicellulose
d
4. Which of these is not a direct treatment on hemicellulose?
a) Cellulase production
b) Hexose fermentation
c) Syngas fermentation
d) Pentose fermentation
c
5. Which of these are acetotropic methanogens?
a) Methanoculleus thermophilicus, Methanosphaera stadtmanii
b) Methanosarcina thermophila, Methanosaeta concilii
c) Methanosarcina thermophila, Methanosphaera stadtmanii
d) Methanoculleus thermophilicus, Methanosarcina thermophila
b
6. Which of these bacteria is a redox mediator in microbial fuel cells?
a) Pseudomonas aeruginosa
b) Geothrix ferementans
c) Desulfuromonas acetoxidans
d) Shewanella oneidenensis
b
7. Which has the correct descending order of Inhibitory Capacity?
a) Propionic > Butyric > Acetic > Lactic
b) Propionic > Acetic > Butyric > Lactic
c) Lactic > Butyric > Acetic > Propionic
d) Lactic > Acetic > Butyric > Propionic
a
8. Which of the following is an undesirable microbe in endogenic silage microorganism with simple sugar as the substrate?
a) Homolactic
b) Propionic acid bacteria
c) Heterolactic
d) Yeast
d
9. Which of following is not a focus in conversing microbial communities to biofuel?
a) Substrate Flexibility
b) High productivity
c) Higher number of products in one process
d) Tolerance to stress
c
