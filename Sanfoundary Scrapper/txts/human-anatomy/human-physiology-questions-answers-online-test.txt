1. FSH and LH are collectively known as _______
a) Neurohomones
b) Antistress hormones
c) Gonadotrophic hormone
d) Emergency hormone
c
2. Which of the following is not a heredity disease?
a) Hemophilia
b) Cretinism
c) Cystic fibrosis
d) Thalassemia
b
3. Which of the following hormone is responsible for ovulation?
a) LH
b) FSH
c) Progesterone
d) Testosterone
a
4. Which of the following is a part of endocrine gland?
a) Pars radiata
b) Brunner’s gland
c) Juxtaglomerulus
d) Crypts of Lieberkuhn
c
5. Who is the father of endocrinology?
a) R H Whittakar
b) Pasteur
c) Thomas Addison
d) Einthoven
c
6. Stress hormone is __________
a) Oxytocin
b) Adrenaline
c) Vasopressin
d) Sex hormone
b
7. Which of the following is responsible for sleep cycle movements?
a) Melatonin
b) Dopamine
c) Serotin
d) Adrenaline
a
8. Which part of the ovary of mammals acts as endocrine gland after copulation?
a) Stroma
b) Graaffian follicle
c) Vitelline membrane
d) Germinal epithelium
b
9. Which of the following hormone is a modified amino acid?
a) Progesterone
b) Estrogen
c) Prostaglandins
d) Epinephrine
d
10. Ecdysone is secreted by __________
a) Insects
b) Nematodes’
c) Polychaetes
d) Algae
a
11. Which hormone produces a calorigenic effect?
a) FSH
b) Thyroxine
c) Insulin
d) Adrenaline
a
12. Which of the following hormone is a steroid?
a) Epinephrine
b) Thyroxine
c) Estrogen
d) Prostaglandins
c
13. Deficiency of vasopressin causes ________
a) Goitre
b) Diabetes mellitus
c) Myxoedema
d) Diabetes insipidus
d
