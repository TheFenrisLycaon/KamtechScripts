1. Which part of the nervous system controls reflex actions?
a) Vermis
b) Pons
c) Spinal cord
d) Corpus callosum
d
2. The electrical potential difference between outside and inside of a nerve axon before excitation is known as ______________
a) Resting potential
b) Action potential
c) Spike potential
d) Reaction potential
a
3. Which set of ions are necessary for the nerve impulse conduction?
a) Sodium and potassium ions
b) Sodium and calcium ions
c) Calcium and potassium ions
d) Sodium and magnesium ions
a
4. Reflex arc consists of ________
a) Motor nerve
b) Sensory nerve
c) Both motor and sensory nerve
d) Mixed nerve
c
5. Dorsal root of spinal nerves contains  ________
a) Effectors neurons
b) Stimulators
c) Detector neurons
d) Receptor neurons
d
6. Reflex action is simplest form of response in  _______
a) Simple animals
b) Higher animals
c) Smaller animals
d) Lowest animals
b
7. Which of the following is not involved in voluntary actions?
a) Math neuron
b) Relay neuron
c) Sensory neuron
d) Synapse
c
8. Nerve impulse is conducted toward the cell body through ___________
a) Motor neurons
b) Nerve fibers
c) Axon
d) Dendrons
d
9. Stimuli are also known as __________
a) Receptors
b) Nerve impulse
c) Biosensors
d) Detectors
a
10. Blinking of an eye is an example of ___________
a) Spinal reflux
b) Cranial refluxes
c) Relay neurons
d) Spinal refluxes
b
