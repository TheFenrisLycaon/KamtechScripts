1. Capacitive transducers can be used by _______________
a) Measuring change in distance between plates
b) Measuring change in area of plates
c) Change in a dielectric material
d) All of the mentioned
d
2. Capacitive transducers cannot be used as strain gauges.
a) True
b) False
b
3. Which of the following is correct for the capacitive transducer?
a) Capacitive strain gauges
b) Capacitive tachometers
c) Capacitive pressure transducer
d) All of the mentioned
d
4. For a material capacitance increases with _____________
a) Decrease in area of plates, all other factors constant
b) Increase in distance between plates, all other factors constant
c) Decrease in distance between plates, all other factors constant
d) None of the mentioned
c
	Where,
εr represents dielectric constant
		 	A is the area of plate
 		 	d is the distance between plates.
5. Which of the following is correct for moisture transducers?
a) Dielectric constant of pure water greater than other materials
b) Dielectric constant of pure water much less than other materials
c) Dielectric constant of pure water and of other materials are equal
d) None of the mentioned
a
6. Which of the following device is used for measuring relative humidity?
a) Capacitive pressure transducer
b) Hygrometer
c) Capacitive strain transducer
d) Capacitive moisture transducer
b
7. Which of the following quantities cannot be measured by capacitive transducers?
a) Displacement
b) Speed
c) Moisture
d) None of the mentioned
d
8. Which of the following have high relative permittivity?
a) Bakelite
b) Marble
c) Paraffin
d) All of the mentioned
b
9. Capacitive microphone is an application of ______________
a) Capacitive displacement transducer
b) Capacitive moisture transducer
c) Hygrometer
d) None of the mentioned
a
10. Thermometers are not possible using a capacitive transducer.
a) True
b) False
b
