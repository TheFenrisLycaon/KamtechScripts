1. Which of the following is a dynamic hardness test?
a) Brinell
b) Poldi
c) Knoop
d) Vickers
b
2. What is the standard height of impact for shore test?
a) 125 mm
b) 250 mm
c) 500 mm
d) 750 mm
b
3. What is a weight of hammer in shore test?
a) 2.0 gm
b) 2.3 gm
c) 2.4 gm
d) 3.0 gm
c
4. As the height of rebound enhances the material’s hardness ___________
a) Increases
b) Decreases
c) First Increases and then decreases
d) Remains constant
a
5. Specimen less than 1 kg must be clamped because of ________
a) Elastic recovery
b) Penetration
c) Inertial effect
d) Personal error
c
6. What is a disadvantage of shore test?
a) Very slow
b) Deep indentation
c) Clamping problem
d) Heavy machinery
c
7. Which test should be preferred for finished lathe machine bed?
a) Brinell
b) Poldi
c) Shore
d) Rockwell
c
8. What is the height of Poldi apparatus?
a) 10 mm
b) 100 mm
c) 150 mm
d) 250 mm
c
9. What is the disadvantage of Poldi hardness test?
a) Expensive
b) Not portable
c) Not accurate
d) Can’t perform on heavy machinery
c
10. Poldi hardness test gives a value of ________ also along with dynamic hardness.
a) Yield strength
b) Tensile strength
c) Toughness
d) Young’s modulus
b
11. Tensile strength is given in tonnes per square inch in Poldi test.
a) True
b) False
a
12. Hardness of heavy components can’t be measured by Poldi test.
a) True
b) False
b
