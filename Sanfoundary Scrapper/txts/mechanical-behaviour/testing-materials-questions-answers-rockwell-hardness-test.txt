1. Which hardness test is most widely used in the US?
a) Brinell
b) Vickers
c) Shore
d) Rockwell
d
2. Rockwell test utilizes ___________ a measure of hardness.
a) Load
b) Depth of indentation
c) Diameter of indentation
d) Time of loading
b
3. A minor load of _________ is applied to seat the specimen in the Rockwell test.
a) 1 kg
b) 5 kg
c) 10 kg
d) 150 kg
c
4. What is a limitation of the Rockwell test?
a) Slow speed
b) Personal error
c) Bigger size of indentation
d) Different indenters
d
5. What minimizes the amount of surface preparation in Rockwell test?
a) Minor load
b) Indenter surface
c) Indenter geometry
d) Heat-treatment
a
6. How much penetration is indicated by each division in dial in Rockwell test?
a) 0.2 mm
b) 0.02 mm
c) 0.002 mm
d) 0.0002 mm
c
7. Hardened steel is tested on the C scale with ________ indenter and a _______ kg major load.
a) Diamond, 100
b) Diamond, 150
c) 1.6 mm steel ball, 100
d) 3.2 mm steel ball, 150
b
8. Which scale is used to test softer materials?
a) A
b) B
c) C
d) D
b
9. What is the hardness of low brass?
a) HRB 40
b) HRB 55
c) HRC 55
d) HRC 60
b
10. What is the hardness of martensite?
a) 40 HRB
b) 64 HRB
c) 40 HRC
d) 64 HRC
d
11. In A scale, steel ball indenter is used.
a) True
b) False
b
12. Diamond indenter is known as ‘Brale indenter’.
a) True
b) False
a
