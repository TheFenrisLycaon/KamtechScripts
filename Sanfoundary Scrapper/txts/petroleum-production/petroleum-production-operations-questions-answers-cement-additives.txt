1. At 48°F Class A cement requires 48 hrs to reach a compressive strength of 500 psi. What does it mean?
a) The WOC is 500 psi
b) The WOC is 48 hrs
c) The WOC is 48°F
d) The WOC is 24 hrs
b
2. Densified cement slurries (using a friction reducer) have faster strength build up.
a) True
b) False
a
3. Sodium Chloride in low concentration accelerates the cement setting.
a) True
b) False
a
4. Cal-Seal is a high-strength controlled setting _____
a) Gypsum Cement
b) Portland Cement
c) Gypsum+ Portland cement
d) Permafrost Cement
a
5. What type of retarder is Calcium Lignosulfonate (HR means Halliburton retarder)?
a) HR-4 lignin type
b) HR-20 gypsum type
c) HR-13 gypsum type
d) HR- 6L lignox type
a
6. Which of the following is not suitable weighting material?
a) Hematite Ore
b) Barite
c) Ottawa Sand
d) Bentonite
