1. The developments in the field of concrete mix design gave compressive strength ranging between ___________
a) 30-70
b) 70-100
c) 35-45
d) 30-80
b
2. High strength concrete resists ___________
a) Levelling
b) Bursting
c) Tangent moments
d) Trapezoidal moments
b
3. In high strength concrete, high modulus of elasticity in the beam is due to ___________
a) Elastic and creep strains
b) Principle tensile stresses
c) Reinforced beams
d) Vibration
a
4. Which of the following is one of the standard codes used for designing high strength concrete mixes?
a) BS 8110-1985
b) BS 883-1974
c) BS 8113-1986
d) BS 886-1976
a
5. Which of the following is the Indian code used for high strength concrete mixes?
a) IS 1343-1980
b) IS 10263-1982
c) IS 384-1980
d) IS 457-1979
a
6. The cube strength of concrete required for both pre tensioning and post tensioning according to Indian standard codes are ___________
a) 30 and 20
b) 15 and 25
c) 40 and 30
d) 45 and 35
d
7. The full form of ACI method for mix design proportion is ___________
a) American committee institute
b) American council institute
c) American concrete institute
d) American cement institute
c
8. In which method, high strength concrete mixes are designed by relating compressive strength to reference number?
a) Erntroy and Shack locks
b) American concrete institute method
c) British DOE method
d) Indian standard code method
a
9. The water cement ratio used for British DOE method is ___________
a) 0.7
b) 0.5
c) 0.9
d) 0.7
b
10. The degree of workability of Indian standard method is ___________
a) 0.80
b) 0.50
c) 0.60
d) 0.20
a
