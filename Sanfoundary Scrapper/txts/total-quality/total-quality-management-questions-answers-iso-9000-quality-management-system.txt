1. According to ISO, the quality system is the organizational structure, responsibilities, procedures, processes, and resources for implementing quality management.
a) True
b) False
a
2. Which of the following must not be a characteristic of a quality system?
a) It must be well-understood
b) Products or services actually do satisfy customer expectations
c) Emphasis on problem prevention
d) It must be ineffective
d
3. A quality system is involved in all phases from initial identification to final satisfaction of requirements and meeting customer satisfaction.
a) True
b) False
a
4. Which of the following is not an advantage of implementing a quality system that conforms to ISO standards?
a) Improvement in employee involvement
b) Improvement in housekeeping
c) Improvement in customer satisfaction
d) Inefficient decision making
d
5. Which of the following cannot be considered a reason for implementing a quality system that conforms to ISO standards?
a) Improvement in safe working
b) Reduction in customer complaints
c) Increased inspection efforts
d) Decreased inspection efforts
c

b) Improving the efficiency of processes
c) Continuous improvement
d) Organizing processes, improving the efficiency of processes, and continuous improvement

a) ISO 9000
b) ISO 9001
c) ISO 14000
d) ISO 9004

a) ISO 19011:2018
b) ISO 9000:2015
c) ISO 9001:2015
d) ISO 9004:2018

a) ISO 19011:2018
b) ISO 9000:2015
c) ISO 9001:2015
d) ISO 9004:2018

a) ISO 9000:1987
b) ISO 9000:2000
c) ISO 19011:2018
d) ISO 9000:2015

a) ISO 19011:2018
b) ISO 9000:2015
c) ISO 9001:2015
d) ISO 9004:2018
