1. Why are Spot speed studies conducted?
a) To record the distribution of vehicle speeds
b) To record the distribution of time taken to reach a destination
c) To design signals
d) To determine design speed
a
2. On a freeway, spot speed studies are not conducted if the traffic volumes exceed _____
a) 700-1000 veh/hr/ln
b) 500-600 veh/hr/ln
c) 750-1000 veh/hr/ln
d) 100-200 veh/hr/ln
c
3. The mean speed of all the vehicles crossing a location is known as ________
a) Standard deviation
b) Median
c) Time mean speed
d) Pace
c
4. Which of the following is a method to measure spot speed?
a) Long Base method
b) Aerial Photography
c) Inductive loop detector
d) Weigh In Motion (WIM) sensor
a
5. What is the disadvantage of Direct Timing procedure?
a) Parallax effect
b) Uneconomical
c) Complicated
d) Inaccurate results
a
6. Which of the following is not a procedure of Long base method?
a) Direct Timing procedure
b) Enoscope
c) Pressure contact tubes
d) Radar speed meters
d
7. Which of the following is a disadvantage of Pressure contact tubes?
a) Low initial cost
b) Simple to install
c) Easy maintenance
d) Tubes are visible which affects the driver’s behaviour
d
8. Which of the following is an advantage of Enoscope?
a) Tubes are visible which affects the driver’s behaviour
b) Parallax effect
c) Inaccurate results
d) Eliminates Parallax error
d
9. Which of the following instrument works on the Doppler principle?
a) Enoscope
b) Radar speed meter
c) Pressure contact tubes
d) Time lapse camera
b
10. The average difference between observed speeds and time mean speed is _____
a) 85th percentile speed
b) Median
c) Standard deviation
d) Pace
c
